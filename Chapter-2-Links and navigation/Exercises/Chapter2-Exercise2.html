About

Shark Tale is a 2004 American animated comedy film from DreamWorks. The film was directed by Vicky Jenson, Bibo Bergeron, and Rob Letterman, from a screenplay written by Letterman and Michael J.



Synopsis

So, there's this little fish named Oscar, bit of a dreamer but stuck in a dead-end job cleaning whales' tongues. He's not exactly living the high life. Anyway, things get wild when the son of a notorious shark mob boss gets accidentally whacked, and Oscar ends up taking credit for it!

This throws him into the deep end (pun intended) because the boss, a real tough guy voiced by Robert De Niro, is after revenge. Oscar teams up with the shark's surprisingly vegetarian brother, Lenny (think Jack Black as a peace-loving shark), to keep up the whole "shark slayer" act. It's hilarious chaos as Oscar tries to navigate this crazy underworld while Lenny wrestles with his non-predatory urges.

Will Oscar come clean? Will Lenny learn to, you know, actually eat meat? You'll have to dive into the movie to find out! It's a fun ride with some great characters and a whole lot of undersea adventure.



Cast

Here are some of the main cast members of Shark Tale:

  • Will Smith as Oscar, the fast-talking fish who gets tangled up with the mob.
  • Robert De Niro as Don Lino, the tough mob shark boss.
  • Renée Zellweger as Angie, Oscar's love interest.
  • Jack Black as Lenny, Don Lino's surprisingly vegetarian shark son.
  • Angelina Jolie as Lola, a seductive gold-digger fish who uses Oscar.
  • Martin Scorsese as Sykes, Don Lino's lawyer (voiced by the famous director himself!).

The Godfather, Goodfellas, The Irishman, and Shark Tale all have two things in common: Robert De Niro and the mafia… But only one of these films also features animated sharks, so I ask you, which film is the real cinema classic?

DreamWorks Studio and Oscar-nominated (I’m not joking!) 2004 feature Shark Tale is an underrated masterpiece, despite what any haters say! It's got everything you could ever ask for: humour, a strong moral centre, sexy fish, and even the mob. No matter how old you are, there is something to grab your attention in this film! Each time you watch it, a new joke pops up or a quote sticks in your head. It's a top tier cinematic experience that should get more love!

Shark Tale’s star-studded voice cast is quite the catch, too… The cast have perfect comedic chemistry and they bring their real-life personas to their animated fishy counterparts. From Will Smith, Jack Black, Robert De Niro, Angelina Jolie, Bob Marley’s son and even Martin Scorsese!

At every turn, there are fish puns that you can’t help but smile and roll your eyes at like the seahorse race track, Coral-Cola billboards, and the ever-informative news reporter, Katie Current… The soundtrack, which keeps the momentum going through the whole film, is a perfect early 2000s gem of a track that includes many hit songs, and a Hans Zimmer score to round it all out.

Yes, it's technically a gangster film… Yes, it’s FULL of fish puns! But the messages of accepting who you are and the damage that lying can do are timeless. Through tense trials and the power of real friendship, the characters learn to accept themselves and live in their truths. It's powerful and raw and more importantly, a lesson people of all ages can resonate with.
